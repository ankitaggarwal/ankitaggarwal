# Once wrote code. Once started a company. Now does both, differently. 🧩

Once upon a time, a kid figured out that if you typed the right words into a box, the box did things. Silly things. A blinking cursor. A little square that slid across the screen. It felt like magic. He never really got over it. ✨

He wrote code for phones in a few hundred million pockets 📱, then left to build a company. Alone 📦. Learned that a business is just software you can't `git revert`. These days he does product for a living, and still writes the code on nights and weekends, because the magic never wore off.

I'm **Ankit**. I build small, opinionated things and run most of them on a computer under my desk. 👇

---

## 🤖 I run my own AI. At home. On 16 GB of RAM.

There's a Mac mini in the corner named **Cleo**. It quietly runs three models, all local: **Gemma** to think, **Parakeet** to listen, **EmbeddingGemma** to remember, all stitched behind one private API I reach over Tailscale. About 30 tokens/sec when it writes, ~310 when it reads, and exactly **$0.00 per token**.

Almost everything below leans on it. No invoice from a frontier lab. Just electricity and stubbornness. ⚡

## 🛠️ Things I've made (and actually use)

🎧 **[Podcast Search](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/podsearch.space)** · [podsearch.space](https://podsearch.space)
I trusted a dozen podcasts more than I trusted search engines, so I made them searchable. Ask a question, get an answer built *only* from real transcripts, and every sentence comes with a receipt you can check: the episode, the timestamp. A model that synthesizes, never invents. (It also has a tiny pair of blinking eyes. 👀)

🧍 **[Quiet Humans](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/quiethumans.fyi)** · [quiethumans.fyi](https://quiethumans.fyi)
A search engine for the indie web: the people building odd, wonderful things on their own little websites, not the ones farming followers. It crawls personal sites, a local Gemma decides who is *actually making things* (a heat printer, a 3D-printed ring? yes. a hot-take thread? no.), and then a human says the final yes. **Discovery is generous; judgment is deliberate.**

🦾 **[Reachy Mini Agent](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/everything-agent)**
A desk robot that listens, answers, and *wiggle-dances its feelings*. Happy, it perks up. Curious, it tilts its head. Wrong, it shakes "no" with its antennas. The choreography runs in sync with its voice, and it hears its wake word fully on-device.

☎️ **[HowzYour.Day](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/howzyour.day)** · [howzyour.day](https://howzyour.day)
You call a phone number, talk through your day, hang up. Next time, it remembers you. Not a therapist (it is forbidden from ever saying "I understand"), more the friend who says "ha, nice" and sits with the quiet. Your phone number *is* your account.

📰 **[Cleo Daily](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/cleo-daily)**
**Read hundreds. Keep a tenth. Print fourteen.** An engine that reads my feeds every morning, throws away 90% of them, and lays out the survivors as a print-ready magazine for an ink-tank printer 🖨️ - because the cure for the infinite feed is a thing that physically cannot scroll back.

🧱 **[Lil Cleo](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/lil-cleo)** - *meet Brick*
This is **Brick**. He's a LEGO minifig who lives on my dock and quietly watches my Mac's vital signs. CPU spikes? He sweat-drops. The machine overheats? His hair *actually catches fire* 🔥. It's 2 a.m. and I'm still typing? He gently offers a "maybe rest?" He has 22 real moods and walks with motion-capture borrowed from a real human actor, shrunk to minifig size. I built him from scratch in headless Blender, with zero art skills and a lot of help from a robot.

📋 **[CopyStack](https://github.com/ankitaggarwal/getcopystack.xyz)** · [getcopystack.xyz](https://getcopystack.xyz)
A clipboard that's a stack, not a history. Copy a few things in order, paste them back one at a time, each one vanishing as it leaves. The constraint *is* the feature.
